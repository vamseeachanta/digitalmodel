1. Overview

The OCIMF Mooring Equipment Guidelines provide dimensionless force coefficients used to compute wind and current loads on moored vessels for mooring design (MBL sizing, fairlead arrangement, fender selection). This explorer renders all digitized Annex A figures and supports parametric comparisons across the principal variables.

2. How to use these coefficients

2.1 Wind force formulation (OCIMF MEG3 §3.2 / MEG4 Ch.3)

Wind forces and yaw moment are computed from coefficient lookups on a vessel-specific reference area and projected dimensions:

Fxw = (½) · ρa · Vw² · AT · Cxw(θw)

Fyw = (½) · ρa · Vw² · AL · Cyw(θw)

Mxyw = (½) · ρa · Vw² · AL · LBP · Cxyw(θw)

where ρa ≈ 1.226 kg/m³, Vw is wind speed (10 m reference), AT is transverse projected area, AL is lateral projected area, LBP is length-between-perpendiculars.

2.2 Current force formulation

Fxc = (½) · ρw · Vc² · LBP · T · Cxc(θc, WD/T)

Fyc = (½) · ρw · Vc² · LBP · T · Cyc(θc, WD/T)

Mxyc = (½) · ρw · Vc² · LBP² · T · Cxyc(θc, WD/T)

where ρw ≈ 1025 kg/m³, Vc is current velocity (apply K-factor correction from Figure A16 if free-stream velocity is referenced), LBP is length-between-perpendiculars, T is draft.

Key parameter: WD/T = water depth / draft ratio. Coefficient magnitude is highly sensitive to shallow water (low WD/T) and lateral coefficient especially.

2.3 Step-by-step usage

  1. Pick the right figure by vessel class and condition:
    • Tanker, loaded → A5–A9 (Cxc), A10 (Cyc), A11 (Cxyc)
    • Tanker, ballasted at 40% T → A12 (Cxc), A13 (Cyc), A14 (Cxyc)
    • Gas carrier (LNG/LPG) → A17 (Cxw), A18 (Cyw), A19 (Cxyw) — wind only; current coefs from tanker tables with appropriate area
    • Current velocity correction → A16 (K factor)
  2. Determine secondary axis value:
    • For current coefficients: compute WD/T = water depth / draft. Pick the figure curve at the closest WD/T (or interpolate between).
    • For wind coefficients (gas carrier): pick tank type — prismatic membrane or spherical (Moss).
    • For loaded-tanker Cxc (A5–A9): pick hull bow shape — cylindrical or conventional.
  3. Look up coefficient at the heading angle of interest (0° = bow-on, 90° = beam, 180° = stern-on).
  4. Compute force/moment using formulas above and vessel-specific reference areas.
  5. Sum wind + current contributions in the vessel-fixed reference frame.

2.4 Sign conventions and angle reference

OCIMF uses a vessel-fixed coordinate system. Heading angle θ is measured from the bow (0°) clockwise toward the starboard beam (90°), stern (180°), port beam (270°). Cyw symmetric vessels have Cx antisymmetric and Cy symmetric about θ=180°, so the published tables span 0°–180° only.

Coefficient signs reflect force direction in the vessel frame:

  • Positive Cxw/Cxc: force pointing toward stern (head wind/current pushes stern-ward when ahead of beam)
  • Positive Cyw/Cyc: force pointing toward starboard (transverse wind from port produces starboard force)
  • Positive Cxyw/Cxyc: bow-toward-starboard yaw moment

2.5 K-factor correction (Figure A16)

When the available current measurement is a free-stream / undisturbed-flow velocity (e.g., from a metocean buoy upstream of the vessel), the actual velocity at vessel midships is reduced due to blockage. The K-factor scales the design current velocity:

Vc, design = K(% load, WD/T) · Vc, free-stream

K depends on % full-load condition (0–100%) and WD/T. For deep water (WD/T ≥ 3), K is close to 1.0 across all loading conditions. For shallow water (WD/T ≤ 1.2), K drops to ~0.9 at light load.

These cross-cutting views surface the principal sensitivities engineers most often need to inspect when selecting design coefficients for a specific mooring analysis.

Loaded-tanker Cxc (A5–A9, cylindrical bow only) is overlaid against the A12 ballasted envelope. Useful for sizing for the conservative load case across vessel-condition uncertainty.

Gas Carrier wind: Prismatic vs Spherical tank (all three wind coefficients)

All three wind coefficients (Cxw, Cyw, Cxyw) overlaid for both tank types. Spherical tanks typically show larger Cyw magnitudes at beam wind.

Hull bow shape effect — Ballast Cyc (A13)

Direct comparison of how bow shape selection changes ballast lateral drag at a fixed WD/T. Conventional bows appear at the smaller WD/T values only.

Polar — Tanker Loaded Cyc by WD/T (A10)

Polar projection emphasizes the directional pattern of lateral current drag. The radial extent at WD/T=1.05 vastly exceeds deep-water — visual confirmation that shallow water dominates lateral mooring loads. All Cyc values in A10 are positive in OCIMF's vessel-fixed convention, so all traces appear solid/circle (force points to +Y starboard for a starboard-incidence current heading).

Polar — Gas Carrier Cyw by tank type (A18)

Gas carrier lateral wind drag polar — prismatic and spherical curves are similar at most headings; differences cluster near beam wind. All Cyw values are negative across 0-180° (all traces dashed/×) because OCIMF tabulates only the starboard-incidence half; the negative sign indicates lateral force pointing to port. For port-incidence wind (180-360°), the published curves are mirrored with sign flipped (Cyw becomes positive, force to starboard) — vessel port/starboard symmetry.
