Methodology

This analysis provides in-service decision support for aging offshore structures: given years in service, original installed anode mass, the most recent measured protection potential, and present coating-breakdown condition, it estimates the remaining anode life and the retrofit anode mass required to extend protection to a 40-year target design life. Every engineering quantity is computed by the digitalmodel cathodic_protection modules implementing DNV-RP-B401 — this report is the screening / presentation layer over those modules.

Present Current Demand

The aged structure's present mean current demand is computed by marine_structure_cp.marine_structure_current_demand from the DNV-RP-B401 Table 10-1 design current densities, scaled by each exposure zone's surface area and its present coating-breakdown factor.

Remaining Life (consumed-vs-capacity)

Mass consumed to date is Mconsumed = Imean × t × 8760 ÷ (capacity × utilisation); the remaining usable mass drives the remaining anode life, liferem = Mrem × capacity × utilisation ÷ (Imean × 8760), with capacity = 2000 A·h/kg (Al-Zn-In), utilisation = 0.90. This is computed by anode_depletion.calculate_remaining_life and marine_structure_cp.retrofit_assessment.

Retrofit Sizing & Triggers

retrofit_assessment compares remaining anode life against the remaining design life to the target and sizes the additional anode mass for any shortfall, and flags an urgent retrofit when the measured potential is above the protection threshold (-0.800 V vs Ag/AgCl).

Depletion Profile & Inspection

anode_depletion.generate_depletion_profile projects remaining mass year-by-year to end-of-life, and recommend_inspection_interval sets the inspection interval, type and urgency from the depletion percentage.

Applicable Codes & Standards

  • DNV-RP-B401 (2017) — Cathodic Protection Design (current densities Table 10-1; anode sizing/retrofit §7.7, §10.8)
  • ISO 15589-2 (2004) — Cathodic Protection of Pipeline Systems, anode mass requirements §8.5
  • NACE SP0176 — Corrosion Control of Submerged Areas of Offshore Steel Structures

Assumptions & Limitations

  • All engineering computed by digitalmodel.cathodic_protection (DNV-RP-B401 (May 2021)); this demo does not reimplement DNV-RP-B401 math
  • Present mean current demand from marine_structure_cp.marine_structure_current_demand (DNV-RP-B401 Table 10-1 densities x area x present coating-breakdown factor)
  • Remaining anode life and retrofit sizing from retrofit_assessment + anode_depletion.calculate_remaining_life (consumed-vs-capacity over the 40-yr target design life)
  • Original installed anode mass per structure is a representative screening value (jacket 30000 kg, monopile 11000 kg, PLET 3800 kg), not a project takeoff
  • Anode electrochemical capacity = 2000 A-h/kg (Al-Zn-In), utilisation factor = 0.90, retrofit anode net mass = 200 kg
  • Protection criterion = -0.800 V vs Ag/AgCl; a measured potential above this is treated as under-protected and trips an urgent retrofit
  • Consumption is driven by the mean-current draw over the elapsed years assuming constant demand; measured remaining anode mass (where available from inspection) would override this
  • Inspection intervals follow general industry depletion-band guidance (routine / priority / urgent / critical), not an operator-specific RBI scheme
  • All outputs are preliminary screening estimates requiring review by a qualified CP / integrity engineer
