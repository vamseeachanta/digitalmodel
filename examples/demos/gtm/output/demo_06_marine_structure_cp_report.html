Methodology

This analysis screens sacrificial-anode cathodic-protection (CP) designs for offshore structures across a parametric matrix of structure types, climate regions, design lives, and coating conditions. Every engineering quantity is computed by the digitalmodel marine_structure_cp module implementing DNV-RP-B401 — this report is the screening / presentation layer over that module.

Zone Current Demand

Each structure is decomposed into exposure zones (splash, tidal, submerged, buried-mudline). Per-zone current demand uses the DNV-RP-B401 Table 10-1 design current densities (initial / mean / final) for the selected climate region, scaled by each zone's surface area and coating-breakdown factor: Izone = area × fc × ic. The splash zone is not CP-protected (Table 10-1 density = 0).

Anode Mass & Count

Total anode mass is sized from the mean current demand over the design life (DNV-RP-B401): M = (Imean × lifeyr × 8760) ÷ (capacity × utilisation), with capacity = 2000 A·h/kg (Al-Zn-In), utilisation = 0.90. The anode count is M rounded up to whole 200 kg anodes.

Anode Distribution

Anodes are distributed across the zones in proportion to each zone's fraction of the total final (design) current demand.

Applicable Codes & Standards

  • DNV-RP-B401 (2017) — Cathodic Protection Design (current densities Table 10-1, anode mass Eq. 10.x)
  • NACE SP0176 — Corrosion Control of Submerged Areas of Offshore Steel Structures
  • ISO 12473 (2006) — General Principles of Cathodic Protection in Seawater

Assumptions & Limitations

  • All engineering computed by digitalmodel.cathodic_protection.marine_structure_cp (DNV-RP-B401 (May 2021)); this demo does not reimplement DNV-RP-B401 math
  • Per-zone design current densities from DNV-RP-B401 Table 10-1 (initial/mean/final), selected by exposure zone and climate region
  • Splash zone is not CP-protected (Table 10-1 density = 0); corrosion there is managed by coatings + a corrosion allowance, outside the anode sizing
  • Single anode net mass = 200 kg, electrochemical capacity = 2000 A-h/kg (Al-Zn-In), utilisation factor = 0.90
  • Structure exposure-zone surface areas are representative screening values for the named structure types (4-leg jacket, XL monopile, subsea PLET frame), not project-specific takeoffs
  • Coating-breakdown factors per zone are representative profiles (bare / good / aged / premium); submerged & buried zones are commonly left bare and protected by CP alone
  • Anode count = total mass rounded up to whole anodes; anode distribution is proportional to each zone's share of the final (design) current demand
  • Anode resistance / individual anode current output (Dwight's equation) and electrolyte resistivity-vs-temperature effects are not included in this screening sizing
  • All outputs are preliminary screening estimates requiring review by a qualified CP engineer
