Scope

Objective: Estimate recoverable oil resources (EUR) using Monte Carlo volumetric simulation with probabilistic inputs

Inclusions

  • Original Oil In Place (OOIP) volumetric calculation
  • Estimated Ultimate Recovery (EUR) with recovery factor
  • P10/P50/P90 probabilistic outcomes
  • Deterministic single-point comparison

Exclusions

  • Gas resources and condensate
  • Enhanced oil recovery (EOR) methods
  • Economic cut-off analysis

Limitations

  • Single-phase oil reservoir assumed
  • No inter-parameter correlations modeled

Design Basis

  • SPE/WPC/AAPG/SPEE/SEG PRMS (2018)

Design Life: 30 years field life

Inputs

Water Saturation $S_w$ 0.275 fraction Resistivity log interpretation
Oil Formation Volume Factor $B_{oi}$ 1.2 rb/stb PVT analysis
Recovery Factor $RF$ 0.15 fraction Analog field performance

Methodology

Volumetric Monte Carlo simulation for Original Oil In Place (OOIP) and Estimated Ultimate Recovery (EUR). Each input parameter is sampled from its probability distribution for 10,000 iterations. Results are reported as P10 (high), P50 (best), and P90 (low) estimates per SPE PRMS convention.

Standard: SPE PRMS (Petroleum Resources Management System)

Equation 1: Original Oil In Place
$$\text{OOIP} = 7758 \cdot A \cdot h \cdot \phi \cdot (1 - S_w) / B_{oi}$$
Volumetric OOIP in stock-tank barrels.
(1)
  • $A$ — Drainage area [acres]
  • $h$ — Net pay thickness [ft]
  • $\phi$ — Porosity [fraction]
  • $S_w$ — Water saturation [fraction]
  • $B_{oi}$ — Oil formation volume factor [rb/stb]
Equation 2: Estimated Ultimate Recovery
$$\text{EUR} = \text{OOIP} \times RF$$
Recoverable reserves.
(2)
  • $RF$ — Recovery factor [fraction]
