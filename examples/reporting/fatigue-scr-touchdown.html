Methodology

Fatigue assessment of the SCR touchdown zone (TDZ) using Palmgren-Miner linear damage accumulation. Stress time histories are extracted from global riser analysis at the critical TDZ location. Rainflow cycle counting is applied to obtain stress range histograms. The S-N curve class E is used per DNV-RP-C203 for welded tubular joints in seawater with cathodic protection. A DFF of 10 is applied for safety-critical locations with no planned inspection access.

Assumptions
  1. Linear damage accumulation (Palmgren-Miner) is valid
  2. TDZ location is the critical fatigue hot-spot
  3. Stress time histories from 3-hour sea states are representative
  4. Soil-pipe interaction modelled with non-linear springs
