Scope

Objective: Structural assessment of the GT1R R35 parachute mounting frame under dual parachute deployment at 250 MPH. This is the most severe loading condition for the frame. Determines stress distribution, deformation, and reaction forces to evaluate frame adequacy for dual-chute drag racing applications.

Inclusions

  • 3D beam FEM with 15 nodes, 16 elements, and PIPE cross-section
  • Governing load case: 250 MPH dual chute deployment (52,731 lbs)
  • Von Mises stress distribution and utilisation ratios
  • Reaction forces at all boundary condition nodes (C3 fixed, B1 bolted)

Exclusions

  • Single chute configuration (see CALC-WRK-1364-SC)
  • Nonlinear material behavior (elastic analysis only)
  • Connection detail stress (bolt holes, weld toes)
  • Dynamic/impact loading (static equivalent applied)
  • Buckling analysis

Limitations

  • Linear elastic analysis — utilisation > 1.0 indicates yield exceedance, not actual failure
  • PIPE section assumes uniform wall thickness; local thinning at bends not modeled

Validity Range: Elastic regime only; results valid for utilisation < 1.0

Methodology

3D beam finite element analysis using two methods: 1. CalculiX v2.21 with B32R (3-node quadratic, reduced integration) beam elements and PIPE cross-section — used for stress/displacement results. 2. Independent 3D direct stiffness solver with 6-DOF Euler-Bernoulli beam elements — used for reaction forces and load distribution (verified to machine-precision equilibrium and exact symmetry). Frame geometry: 15 nodes, 16 members from WRK-1360. Boundary conditions: C3 nodes (N7, N10) fixed in all 6 DOFs; B1 nodes (N12, N14) fixed in all 6 DOFs. Drag force applied at coupler pin (N2) in -Y direction.

Standard: 3D Direct Stiffness Method (Euler-Bernoulli beam), verified against CalculiX v2.21

Equation eq1: Von Mises Equivalent Stress
$$\sigma_{vm} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{2}\left[(\sigma_{xx}-\sigma_{yy})^2 + (\sigma_{yy}-\sigma_{zz})^2 + (\sigma_{zz}-\sigma_{xx})^2 + 6(\tau_{xy}^2 + \tau_{yz}^2 + \tau_{zx}^2)\right]}$$
Combined stress criterion for ductile materials
(eq1)
Equation eq2: Utilisation Ratio
$$UR = \frac{\sigma_{vm,max}}{F_y}$$
Ratio of maximum von Mises stress to yield strength; UR > 1.0 indicates yield exceedance
(eq2)
Equation eq3: Equilibrium Check
$$\sum R_y = F_{applied}$$
Sum of vertical reaction forces equals applied load
(eq3)

Conclusions

Adequacy: INADEQUATE — The current tube section (1.75" OD × 0.120" wall, 4130 chromoly normalized) is severely overstressed under dual chute deployment at 250 MPH. Utilisation ratio is 6.66 — the frame exceeds yield capacity by 6.7×. This represents the most demanding loading condition for the frame.

Governing Check: 250 MPH dual chute — UR = 6.66 (max σ_vm = 419,703 psi vs F_y = 63,000 psi). C3 frame rail mounts carry 87.1% of Fy load (22,977 lbs each, |F| = 24,013 lbs each). B1 bolted connections carry 12.9% of Fy load (3,388 lbs each, |F| = 9,776 lbs each). Load distribution is perfectly symmetric (verified by independent solver to machine precision).

Recommendations

  • Current frame section is NOT suitable for dual chute deployment
  • Minimum upgrade path: heat-treated 4130 (Fy = 100 ksi) reduces UR to 4.20 — still fails
  • Larger section required: 2.0" OD × 0.188" wall with HT 4130 for target UR < 1.0
  • Check C3 weld capacity for 24,013 lbs reaction force per side
  • Check B1 bolt group for 9,776 lbs reaction per side — 6× M8 bolts
  • Consider adding diagonal bracing or redesigning load path to reduce C3 concentration

Assumptions
  1. All connections are rigid (welded joints modeled as continuous beam)
  2. B1 bolted connections are fully fixed (6-DOF constrained) — conservative
  3. Load applied as concentrated force at coupler pin node (N2) in -Y direction
  4. Material is homogeneous, isotropic, and linear elastic
  5. Cross-section is constant along each member (uniform PIPE)
  6. Self-weight neglected (small relative to drag forces)
  7. Both chutes deploy simultaneously — total drag treated as single resultant force
