Scope

Objective: Verify that the CalculiX INP writer produces correct B31 beam element input files by comparing CalculiX ccx v2.21 tip deflection results against the closed-form Euler-Bernoulli cantilever beam solution. Acceptance criterion: tip deflection error < 1%.

Inclusions

  • B31 (2-node linear Timoshenko beam) element type
  • Cantilever configuration with end point load
  • Rectangular cross-section (0.1 m × 0.1 m)
  • 10-element mesh convergence

Exclusions

  • B32 (quadratic beam) — validated by element type mapping test only
  • Frame/multi-member structures — deferred to WRK-1364
  • Dynamic, buckling, or nonlinear analysis
  • Non-rectangular cross-sections (CIRC, PIPE)

Validity Range: Linear elastic, small displacements (δ/L < 0.001), slenderness ratio L/h ≥ 10

Methodology

Compare CalculiX B31 Timoshenko beam element results against the Euler-Bernoulli closed-form solution for a cantilever beam with tip load. The B31 element is a 2-node Timoshenko beam that includes transverse shear deformation. For slender beams (L/h >> 10), B31 converges to Euler-Bernoulli. At L/h = 10, a small positive bias (< 1%) is expected due to the additional shear flexibility term κPL/GA. The validation proceeds in 4 steps: 1. Compute second moment of area I = bh³/12 2. Compute analytical tip deflection δ = PL³/(3EI) 3. Run CalculiX ccx v2.21 with the INP writer output 4. Compare δ_ccx vs δ_exact and compute percentage error

Standard: Euler-Bernoulli beam theory — Gere & Timoshenko, Mechanics of Materials, 4th Ed.

Equation 1: Second Moment of Area (rectangular section)
$$I = \frac{b \cdot h^3}{12}$$
Moment of inertia about the neutral axis for a rectangular cross-section.
(1)
  • $I$ — Second moment of area [m⁴]
  • $b$ — Section width [m]
  • $h$ — Section height [m]
Equation 2: Cantilever Tip Deflection (Euler-Bernoulli)
$$\delta = \frac{P \cdot L^3}{3 \cdot E \cdot I}$$
Maximum deflection at the free end of a cantilever beam under a concentrated tip load. Valid for linear elastic, small-displacement conditions.
(2)
  • $\delta$ — Tip deflection [m]
  • $P$ — Concentrated tip load [N]
  • $L$ — Beam length [m]
  • $E$ — Young's modulus [Pa]
  • $I$ — Second moment of area [m⁴]
Equation 3: Percentage Error
$$\varepsilon = \frac{|\delta_{ccx} - \delta_{exact}|}{\delta_{exact}} \times 100$$
Relative error between CalculiX numerical result and analytical solution.
(3)
  • $\varepsilon$ — Percentage error [%]
  • $\delta_{ccx}$ — CalculiX tip deflection [m]
  • $\delta_{exact}$ — Analytical tip deflection [m]
Equation 4: Timoshenko Shear Correction
$$\delta_{Timoshenko} = \delta_{EB} + \frac{\kappa P L}{G A}$$
B31 includes shear deformation — adds a positive term to Euler-Bernoulli deflection. Explains systematic positive bias in ccx results.
(4)
  • $\kappa$ — Shear correction factor (5/6 for rectangular) [-]
  • $G$ — Shear modulus = E/2(1+ν) [Pa]
  • $A$ — Cross-section area [m²]

Calculations

Step 1: Second moment of area

I = b × h³ / 12 = 0.1 × 0.1³ / 12 = 8.3333 × 10⁻⁶ m⁴
Ref: Eq. 1
  • Second moment of area (I) = 8.3333e-06 m⁴

Step 2: Analytical tip deflection (Euler-Bernoulli)

δ = P × L³ / (3 × E × I) = 1000 × 1.0³ / (3 × 210×10⁹ × 8.3333×10⁻⁶) = 1.9048 × 10⁻⁴ m
Ref: Eq. 2
  • Exact tip deflection (δ_exact) = 0.00019048 m

Step 3: CalculiX B31 simulation

10-element B31 cantilever beam, fixed at root (all 6 DOFs constrained), concentrated load P = 1000 N applied at tip node in Y-direction. ccx v2.21 on dev-secondary. Beam section: RECT 0.1 × 0.1 m, orientation (0,0,1). ccx expanded beam nodes at tip (nodes 92-95) all report dy = 1.9116 × 10⁻⁴ m.
Ref: CalculiX User Manual v2.21, §6.2.10
  • ccx tip deflection (δ_ccx) = 0.00019116 m

Step 4: Percentage error computation

ε = |1.9116×10⁻⁴ − 1.9048×10⁻⁴| / 1.9048×10⁻⁴ × 100 = 0.35%
Ref: Eq. 3
  • Percentage error (ε) = 0.35 %

Step 5: Shear deformation contribution estimate

G = E / 2(1+ν) = 210×10⁹ / 2(1.3) = 80.77 GPa. A = b × h = 0.01 m². δ_shear = κPL/GA = (5/6)(1000)(1.0) / (80.77×10⁹ × 0.01) = 1.03 × 10⁻⁶ m. Shear contribution: 1.03×10⁻⁶ / 1.9048×10⁻⁴ = 0.54%. This accounts for the 0.35% positive bias (actual shear effect is mesh-dependent).
Ref: Eq. 4
  • Shear modulus (G) = 80770000000.0 Pa
  • Shear deflection contribution (δ_shear) = 1.03e-06 m
  • Shear contribution ratio = 0.54 %

Validation

Method: CalculiX B31 tip deflection compared against Euler-Bernoulli closed-form solution δ = PL³/3EI. Result: 1.9116e-04 m vs 1.9048e-04 m (0.35% error). The small positive bias is expected: B31 is a Timoshenko element that includes shear deformation (adds ~κPL/GA to the Euler-Bernoulli deflection). Three additional pure-Python analytical tests verify the formula itself and its scaling properties (L³ and I dependencies).

Conclusions

Adequacy: The CalculiX B31 beam element produces tip deflection within 0.35% of the Euler-Bernoulli analytical solution for a cantilever beam with concentrated tip load. This validates that the INP writer correctly generates: (a) B31 element definitions with proper connectivity, (b) *BEAM SECTION cards with rectangular cross-section dimensions, (c) boundary conditions (fixed-end 6 DOF constraint), (d) concentrated loads (*CLOAD) accepted by ccx v2.21.
