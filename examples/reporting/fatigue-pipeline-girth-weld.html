Methodology

Fatigue assessment of a pipeline girth weld using Palmgren-Miner linear damage accumulation. Stress ranges are derived from a Weibull distribution fitted to long-term hydrodynamic analysis. The S-N curve is selected per DNV-RP-C203 for seawater with cathodic protection. Stress ranges are multiplied by the SCF before entering the S-N lookup.

Standard: DNV-RP-C203 (2021)

Equation 1: S-N Curve
$$\log N = \log \bar{a} - m \log \Delta\sigma$$
Relates number of cycles to failure N to stress range.
(1)
  • $N$ — Number of cycles to failure [cycles]
  • $\bar{a}$ — S-N curve intercept (design basis) [-]
  • $m$ — S-N curve slope (negative inverse) [-]
  • $\Delta\sigma$ — Stress range [MPa]
Equation 2: Palmgren-Miner Damage
$$D = \sum_{i=1}^{k} \frac{n_i}{N_i}$$
Cumulative fatigue damage as sum of cycle ratios.
(2)
  • $D$ — Cumulative fatigue damage ratio [-]
  • $n_i$ — Number of applied cycles in stress block i [cycles]
  • $N_i$ — Number of cycles to failure at stress range i [cycles]
Equation 3: Factored Damage
$$D_{factored} = D \times DFF$$
Factored damage applying the Design Fatigue Factor.
(3)
  • $DFF$ — Design Fatigue Factor [-]

Assumptions
  1. Linear damage accumulation (Palmgren-Miner) is valid
  2. Stress ranges follow a Weibull distribution
  3. No significant corrosion degradation beyond CP assumptions
  4. Weld quality meets DNV fabrication tolerances
  5. Mean stress effects are neglected (conservative)
