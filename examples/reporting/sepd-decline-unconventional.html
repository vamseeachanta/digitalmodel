Methodology

Stretched Exponential Production Decline (SEPD) model applied to an unconventional (shale) well. Unlike Arps hyperbolic decline, SEPD avoids the over-prediction of late-life reserves by using a stretched exponential kernel that naturally converges to finite EUR without an arbitrary b-factor switch. The model is fitted to early production history and extrapolated for long-term forecasting.

Standard: Valko & Lee (2010), SPE 134231

Equation 1: SEPD Rate
$$q(t) = q_i \exp\!\left(-\left(\frac{t}{\tau}\right)^n\right)$$
Production rate at time t.
(1)
  • $q_i$ — Initial production rate [bbl/day]
  • $\tau$ — Characteristic time constant [days]
  • $n$ — Stretching exponent (0 < n <= 1) [-]
Equation 2: SEPD Cumulative Production
$$Q(t) = \frac{q_i \tau}{n}\,\Gamma\!\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)\,P\!\left(\frac{1}{n},\left(\frac{t}{\tau}\right)^n\right)$$
Cumulative production from 0 to t using the lower regularised incomplete gamma function P(a,x).
(2)
  • $Q(t)$ — Cumulative production [bbl]
  • $\Gamma$ — Complete gamma function [-]
  • $P(a,x)$ — Lower regularised incomplete gamma function [-]

Assumptions
  1. Single-phase oil flow; no gas lift or ESP interference
  2. Constant bottomhole pressure throughout forecast period
  3. No refracturing or well intervention modelled
  4. Stretching exponent n is constant over the well life
  5. Type-curve parameters from analogous Permian Basin completions
