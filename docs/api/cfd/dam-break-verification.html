1  Objective

Reproduce the classic 2D collapse of a water column on a rigid horizontal plane and verify the volume-of-fluid (VOF) free-surface machinery — interface capturing (MULES), setFields phase initialisation, gravity-driven p_rgh formulation and the entraining atmosphere boundary — against the canonical experiment of Martin & Moyce (1952). A water column of base width a = 0.1461 m and height 2a (the n² = 2 configuration) is released instantaneously in a plain 8a × 4a tank (no obstacle — the stock tutorial's obstacle is removed to match the experiment). The measured quantity is the dimensionless surge-front position Z = x/a against dimensionless time T = t √(2g/a).

This is the first multiphase case of the suite: the previous four (Blasius plate, cylinder, turbulent plate, NACA0012) are all single-phase. If interFoam cannot reproduce the dam-break surge front, no downstream marine free-surface result can be trusted.

2  Primary result — surge-front position

The computed front tracks the experiment closely in slope (front celerity) from the start; the visible offset at early T is the experiment's finite gate-release time — the diaphragm cannot be removed instantaneously, so the measured front lags the ideal release the CFD implements. The literature-standard correction, applied identically by e.g. the Lethe benchmark of this experiment, is a +0.175 dimensionless time shift; with it, every digitized point agrees within 8.1%.

3  Mass conservation

A volFieldValue function object integrates alpha.water over the domain every time step. The initial volume matches the analytic column volume a · 2a · depth to five digits (the uniform mesh is sized dx = a/36 so the column edges fall exactly on cell faces), and the total drift over the run is 6.4e-07 — four orders of magnitude inside the 1% gate. MULES with MULESCorr keeps the VOF field conservative and bounded.

4  Column collapse

The interface stays sharp (2–3 cells) throughout the collapse; the residual column height at the left wall decays monotonically as the column drains into the surge, consistent with the published collapse behaviour.

6  Conclusion

The interFoam VOF stack reproduces the canonical dam-break benchmark: surge-front kinematics within 8.1% of every digitized Martin & Moyce point (with the standard gate-release correction; 3.0% mean), raw comparison within the documented experimental-release offset, and machine-level mass conservation. The free-surface foundation for the marine validation backlog (waves, green water, sloshing — #1169–#1173, #637–#643) is verified. Suite status: 5/5 verified cases (laminar BL, turbulent RANS BL, transient bluff body, lift-generating airfoil, free-surface VOF).
