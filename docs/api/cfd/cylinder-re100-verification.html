4  Triggering the instability (engineering note)

Why the wake first refused to shed. On a perfectly grid-aligned, symmetric setup the discrete solution preserves the up/down symmetry, so the shedding instability — though physically unstable at Re=100 (above the critical Re≈47) — cannot grow from a symmetric state; the lift stayed identically zero to t>40. Because a circle is rotationally symmetric, tilting the free stream by 5° is the same physical flow rotated — it leaves Cd and St unchanged — but it breaks the grid-aligned numerical symmetry and seeds the instability, which then grows exponentially and saturates by t≈120. Forces are measured along the rotated flow/normal axes, so the reported coefficients are unbiased.

5.1 Strouhal extraction

The shedding frequency is taken from the up-crossings of the lift signal over 5 periods (period 6.044 time units), giving St = 0.1655. A raw FFT of the same window returns 0.175, but its frequency-bin width (1/40 ≈ 0.025) is too coarse to resolve St to better than ~15%; the crossing-based estimate is the reliable one. This distinction is itself a verification of the post-processing, not just the solver.
