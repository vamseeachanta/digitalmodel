Normalized master curve

Dimensionless sloshing frequency Ω₁ = ω₁√(Lc/g) vs fill / slenderness ratio h/Lc

Any tank of a given shape — any size — lies on its curve. Read Ω₁ off the curve at your fill ratio, then the real period is T = 2π√(Lc/g) / Ω₁. Deep-liquid asymptotes: rectangular √π = 1.77, upright cylinder √1.841 = 1.36 (×√2 on a diameter basis).

Horizontal-cylinder and elliptical curves use the equivalent-rectangle method (good to ~10–15% at mid fill; near-full and violent cases are where a VOF free-surface CFD run takes over).

Forced-roll resonance — with vs without

How forced roll reveals the sloshing resonance

Without forced roll, a free-decay CFD run gives the tank's intrinsic first-mode natural period (the point on the master curve). With forced roll, driving the tank at a range of roll periods and measuring the wall run-up traces a resonance curve that peaks at that same natural period — roll excites the mode the free-decay rings down. Response is normalised to each fill's own peak and plotted against the drive period over the natural period, Tdrive/T₁.

Anti-roll tuning — CFD-reduced tuned damper

Tune the tank to your vessel: fill for a target roll period, and the damping it delivers

The forced-roll CFD reduces the tank to a single-DOF tuned damper — an equivalent damping ratio ζ (rising with fill) plus added roll damping/inertia. Tuning is a curve, not a single “best fill”: set the tank length L and fill so the sloshing period f₁(fill) matches the vessel roll period; the fill then sets the damping.

ζ and B₄₄ from the forced-roll CFD (in-phase / quadrature reduction; SloshingCouplingModel). Higher fill → more damping. This is a screening-grade linearization, valid as a design anchor, not a full model: ζ is a config-specific equivalent (not an invariant law), fixed at 4° roll (larger amplitude bends the backbone — softening at these fills, h/L ≥ 0.30; shallow fills harden instead — and raises breaking-driven damping); it is 2D, so it cannot capture 3D rotary/swirl modes near resonance; there are no baffles/screens (damping here is wave-breaking only — real anti-roll tanks tune damping with internals); and roll–sway coupling / roll-axis position are not modelled. Only three fills, so ζ/B₄₄ are coarsely interpolated. See the CFD study for the backbone and full caveats.
