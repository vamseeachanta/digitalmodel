Low-speed manoeuvring & station-keeping explorer

Pick a vessel in the sidebar — from a VLCC to a harbour tug (two are published manoeuvring-benchmark hulls; the rest are class-typical estimates). Answers three operational questions — can I turn within the channel? (turning circle vs the IMO 5L limit), do I still have steerage? (threshold speed vs wind, by loading), and can I hold heading on the engine? (rudder angle to balance the current yaw moment, and the current at which a tug is needed). Drag the controls.

4 · Current load — yaw moment & lateral force vs heading
Method & scope. Screening-level. Turning circle from the Nomoto relation R/L = 1/(K'·δ) (K' calibrated to the Lyster & Knights 1979 sea-trial regression, TD/L ≈ 3.2 laden at 35°); threshold speed from a rudder-vs-beam-wind force balance Umin = (Vw/c)·√(ρair·AL·CY / (ρw·ARe·a·sinδ)); engine-on rudder authority from the Söding/Brix slipstream model (yaw ∝ Crp·Fprop·[1+1/√(1+Cth)]·sinδ); current load from the OCIMF form N = CXYc·½ρVc²·L²·T, Fy = CYc·½ρVc²·L·T. Not a class/IMO compliance proof and not a full MMG/6-DOF model. Per-vessel K' (turning), windage and ballast rudder-emergence are screening estimates derived from each vessel's geometry. Ducted/gate/twin-rudder vessels carry system side force the bare-rudder lift slope under-predicts. Source: naval_architecture/maneuvering_envelope.py and data/rudder_database.yml.
