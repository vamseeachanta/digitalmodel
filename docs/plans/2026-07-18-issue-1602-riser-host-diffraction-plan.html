Source handoff and three engineering envelopes

A producer-owned PublicFloaterSourceRecord will bind source URL commitment, capture date, license/redistribution, content hash, physical identity, loading/particulars/mass evidence, eligibility, ambiguity disposition, and the exact commitment consumed by the host identity.

  1. FloatingHostIdentityEnvelope will bind the source commitment, physical vessel, hull geometry, loading condition, particulars, mass properties, aliases, identity evidence, units/frame, confidence, public eligibility, ambiguity disposition, and complete-envelope commitment.
  2. HydrodynamicResponseEnvelope will bind the exact host/loading/mesh, diffraction target/adapter/projection/run evidence, ordered DOFs, frequency-heading grid, incident-wave basis, first-order complex wave-excitation payload, complex RAO and matrix payloads, units/conventions/frame/reference point, water properties, source, validation, usable domain, fidelity, and complete-envelope commitment.
  3. VesselMotionBoundaryEnvelope will bind the case/wave, host and full response commitment, complex RAO, top-connection frame/reference, rigid-body transform and independent analytical validation, phase/time basis, coupling intent, boundary payload, coverage/domain, unsupported disposition, and complete-envelope commitment. Seven host-motion chain fields—four IDs and three envelope hashes—plus the analysis-contract and selected-request commitments will propagate unchanged through request → bundle → execution request → receipt → engineering result. Each released envelope set will equal, in both directions, the distinct approved envelope references used by released engineering results: no omitted reference and no orphan member will be allowed. Long-form response rows will exactly project and cover the released hydrodynamic-response set.

Public Hugging Face surface

The release will contain exactly 17 configs:

source_registry, transformations, citations, criteria, clean_room_reviews, field_lineage, riser_components, riser_configurations, analysis_cases, execution_attestations, strength_results, operability_results, withheld_sources, floating_hosts, hydrodynamic_responses, hydrodynamic_response_values, and vessel_motion_boundaries.

Response metadata and validation/fidelity will occupy hydrodynamic_responses; viewer-compatible long-form excitation, RAO, and matrix ordinates will occupy hydrodynamic_response_values. Exact response kinds will be total, Froude-Krylov, and diffraction wave excitation; complex RAO; added mass; radiation damping; and restoring. Excitation and RAO rows will carry DOF, frequency, and heading with finite canonical real/imaginary ordinates. Matrix rows will retain their existing kind-specific axes. Inapplicable axes will be null, never sentinels.

Every row will carry an exact physical schema: response and envelope IDs, kind, DOF row/nullable column, frequency index and rad/s value, heading index and radian value, real/nullable-imaginary ordinate, and exact ordinate unit. Kind rules will make the applicable fields non-null and every inapplicable field null. The projection-contract hash will be a minimum binding of the response envelope, so index/value/unit changes cannot evade the release commitment.

For zero-forward-speed diffraction, the incident elevation will be η(x,t)=Re(a exp(i k·(x−x_ref) − i ωt)), with intrinsic ω in rad/s, a=1 m (2 m wave height), and a positive crest at the bound reference point at t=0. The canonical response frame will be body-fixed right-handed x-forward/y-port/z-up. Response heading will be the propagation-toward wave-vector azimuth counterclockwise from body +x on [0,2π). It will map from the inherited ENU clockwise-from-true-north going-to convention as β_body=wrap(ψ_body−θ_global), with body-x global azimuth committed by the incident-wave basis. All response phasors will use Re(X exp(−i ωt)). Loads will be external loads on the body in canonical positive DOFs, with moments about the bound coordinate-frame reference point. Translational/rotational RAOs will use m/m and rad/m; translational/moment excitation will use N/m and N·m/m without dimensional simplification. Only first-order linear excitation will qualify; mean drift and QTFs will be excluded.

Total excitation will be mandatory. Let n be the body-outward unit normal pointing into the fluid. Complex pressure will exert traction −p n on the body, so canonical force will be F_exc=−∫p n dS and excitation moment about the bound reference will be 𝓜_exc=−∫(r−r_ref)×p n dS. Froude-Krylov will use incident pressure in those integrals over the bound mean wetted surface; diffraction will use scattered pressure in the same integrals and surface. Hydrostatic, radiation, mooring, mean-drift, and QTF terms will be excluded. Components will appear together or will both be absent under the envelope-bound wave_excitation_component_availability_sha256; when present, their complex sum will equal total within a citation-backed tolerance. Excitation, RAO, and matrix payload preimages will carry equal execution/run, ordered-DOF, grid, incident-basis, unit, phase/heading, and reference-point commitments; every payload context will also equal the enclosing response envelope's host, loading/mass, hull/mesh/spec, adapter/projection, water, execution/run, DOF/grid/basis, unit/convention, and reference commitments. The coherence oracle will use exact shared coordinates without interpolation. Under Re(X exp(−iωt)), it will use Z(ω)=−ω²(M+A(ω))−iωB(ω)+C after transforming every mixed-unit 6×6 term to the same reference point and ordered DOFs, and will require Z·RAO=excitation within a citation-backed tolerance. M will be the bound rigid-body mass, A added mass, B radiation damping, and C bound hydrostatic restoring; external damping, mooring, or other restoring will require a fresh committed extension. Nested response arrays, private hydrodynamic values, client-derived rows, and native diffraction/riser solver artifacts will not enter the public dataset.
