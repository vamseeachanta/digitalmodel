PE Problem of the Day 5/21/2026 — OOIP volumetrics & recovery

productionupstreamreservoir-engineeringvolumetrics

Problem

A sandstone oil reservoir: areal extent 1,200 acres; net pay 40 ft; average porosity 18%; initial oil saturation 80%; oil formation volume factor 1.25; planned primary recovery factor 22% of OOIP. Assume uniform saturation, fully drained within the area and net pay. (a) Estimate OOIP in stock-tank barrels (STB). (b) Estimate primary recoverable oil (STB). (c) If a secondary project raises the recovery factor to 30%, how many extra STB does that represent vs. primary alone?

Given

area acres1200
net pay ft40
porosity0.18
oil saturation0.8
formation volume factor boi1.25
primary recovery factor0.22
secondary recovery factor0.3

Find

  • (a) OOIP in STB
  • (b) primary recoverable STB at RF 22%
  • (c) incremental STB from RF 22% -> 30%

Method

Volumetric OOIP = 7758*A*h*phi*So/Boi; recoverable = OOIP*RF; uplift = OOIP*(RF2-RF1).

Worked Solution

  1. OOIP = 7758 * 1200 * 40 * 0.18 * 0.80 / 1.25 = 42,898,636.8 STB (~42.9 MMSTB)
  2. (b) primary = 42,898,636.8 * 0.22 = 9,437,700 STB (~9.44 MMSTB)
  3. (c) uplift = 42,898,636.8 * (0.30 - 0.22) = 3,431,891 STB (~3.43 MMSTB)

Answer

a ooip stb: 42898636.8
b primary recoverable stb: 9437700.1
c secondary uplift stb: 3431891.0

Engineer's Note

Volumetric estimate assumes uniform saturation and full areal/vertical drainage; real recovery depends on drive mechanism, sweep efficiency, and well placement. 7758 = bbl per acre-ft.
